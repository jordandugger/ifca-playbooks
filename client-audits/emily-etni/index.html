Masterclass Funnel Analysis

The Masterclass funnel is Fit Emz's most differentiated acquisition mechanism and historically its strongest client source. In Q1–Q2 2026, four masterclasses were run. The core problem is not the concept — offer-view rates among attendees are solid. The problem is attendance rate (11–27%) and the lead-to-book conversion after the event (2.7–10%). The mandatory 1-hour masterclass requirement in the call confirmation process is the identified friction driver suppressing show rate before the setter even gets a chance.

Attendance rate benchmark: IFCA A-tier masterclass = 35%, B-tier = 25%, C-tier = 18%. All four 2026 masterclasses landed in C-tier or below (11–26.7%). The mandatory 1-hour pre-call watch requirement is creating cancellation friction before the event, not just before the call. Removing this barrier is the highest-leverage single change available to the funnel.

Offer-to-book rate (A-tier = 30%): Among those who actually attended and saw the offer, book rates ranged from 25–60% across events — above or at benchmark. This confirms the content works. The problem is upstream: not enough people are attending to convert at the rates the content can produce. Fix attendance, and the masterclass becomes the business's primary growth engine again.

Strategic diagnosis: The masterclass funnel is generating enough interest to collect registrants and show a positive ROAS on its best run (2.32×), but the step-down from registration to attendance to booked call is too steep. At 11–27% attendance on 73–106 registrants per event, 65–90% of paid registrants are evaporating before seeing the offer. The solution path is clear: replace the 1-hour confirmation requirement with a short VSL + objection-handling breakout videos (Jeremy Haynes-style), add a reminder + bribe sequence (Hormozi $5 gift card reciprocity method), and implement a hybrid 1-call close with lead scoring to route self-books more efficiently.

Section 06
Constraint Diagnosis
P1
Show Rate 41% — Mandatory 1-Hour Masterclass Confirmation Killing Show-Ups
The Q1 average show rate of 41% (42% on sales calls, 47% on rapport calls) is the most expensive number in the business. The success coach has identified the root cause: a non-negotiable 1-hour masterclass requirement baked into the call confirmation process for all leads — both set and self-booked. This creates a multi-step friction wall between booking and showing. A lead books a call, gets handed an hour of content to consume before the call, perceives the effort required, and cancels or no-shows. The show rate numbers in the masterclass tracker confirm this: registration → attendance drop-off is 65–90% across all four Q1 masterclasses. The math from the success coach is definitive: fixing show rate from 41% to 70% on the same lead volume triples closes.
Fix (already in progress): Replace the 1-hour masterclass confirmation requirement with a short VSL (Jeremy Haynes-style, 10–15 minutes max) with 2–3 breakout objection-handling videos on the confirmation page. Add automated reminder sequence: 24h, 12h, 3h before call with name/date/time and the number that will call. Add manual "blue iPhone" texts: night before, morning of, 60 min before. Implement the Hormozi $5 gift card reciprocity incentive for qualified leads. Target: show rate above 60% within 45 days of implementation.
P1
6.2% Lead-to-Book Rate — Zero Retargeting + Single Setter Bandwidth
Only 6.2% of leads booked a call in Q1. On DM-funnel and masterclass benchmarks, this range should be 7–10%. The success coach identified two drivers: setter bandwidth (Amani as primary setter is managing both DM funnel leads and masterclass follow-up single-handedly) and the complete absence of retargeting creative. The account is spending $26K/quarter in ad spend generating warm audiences — video viewers, profile visitors, masterclass non-converters — and then running zero ads against them. 93.8% of paid leads never reach a live conversation.
Fix: (1) Second setter already being hired — prioritize onboarding and scripting for Amani's handoff protocol. (2) Implement hybrid 1-call close with app grading: score leads 1–5, route highest scores directly to a 1-call sales calendar (skip triage), route mid-scores to triage, dequeue lowest scores. (3) Launch MOF retargeting ad set targeting 50%+ video viewers and profile visitors from the last 30 days. This warm pool is already paid for — not capturing it is the highest unaddressed revenue leak in the account.
P2
Masterclass Attendance Rate 11–27% vs. 35% A-Tier Benchmark
Across four Q1/early-Q2 masterclasses, attendance ranged from 11% (Mar 11 event) to 26.7% (Feb 23 event) — consistently below the A-tier 35% benchmark. Registrants are converting, but the gap between registered and attended is absorbing the bulk of the event's revenue potential. The Feb 23 event showed 10% lead-to-book (best in the tracker) with 26.7% attendance — confirming that when people show up, the conversion path works. The problem is making people show up.
Fix: Build dedicated show-up creative as described in the Entity Build Queue priority #1. Combine with a pre-event DM automation sequence (registered → 3-message sequence teasing a specific result or insight from the masterclass → reminder message with the "here's what to have ready before you join" hook). The masterclass ROAS could approach 3–5× from its current 0–2.3× range if attendance lifts to the 30–35% range.
P2
CPL $10.68 — Marginally Above $10 Target, Marketing Spend Too High Relative to Revenue
The Q1 CPL of $10.68 sits just above the $10 IFCA benchmark — marginally failing. More critically, the marketing margin averaged 27.9% in Q1 (Jan 24.9%, Feb 34.3%, Mar 27.3%) against a 10–20% target. February's 34.3% marketing margin is particularly elevated. At $31.6K average monthly revenue, spending $7,050+/month on marketing (including ad spend and platform costs) is outside the acceptable range. The business is spending too much per lead relative to its conversion rate — which circles back to the show rate and close rate problems. Fix those, and the marketing margin normalizes without cutting spend.
Fix: The marketing margin will self-correct as show rate and close rate improve — CPA drops from $1,710 to $614 per the success coach's math, which brings marketing margin well within the 10–20% target range. CPL optimization via creative testing (new hook variations on the career-burnout angle, which is the highest-performing) is a secondary lever. Do not cut ad spend to fix marketing margin — fix conversion to earn the spend back.
P2
FE→BE at 13.3% — Structural Limitation, Not a Sales Problem
The 13.3% FE→BE conversion is dramatically below the 50% IFCA benchmark. However, the success coach provides important context: the majority of frontend contracts are 9–12 month deals, and a large share are financed. A client on a 9-month frontend contract is not eligible or ready for backend ascension within a Q1 measurement window.
