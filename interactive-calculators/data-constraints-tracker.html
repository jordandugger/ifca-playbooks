The Constraint Hierarchy

The tracker diagnoses your business across four funnel stages and assigns a priority level to each. Fix P1 before anything else — always.
P1 — Critical
Primary Constraint
The single bottleneck limiting all downstream results. Everything else is noise until this is fixed. You will know your P1 the moment the tracker runs.
P2 — Important
Secondary Constraint
The second biggest drag on your numbers. Fix this after P1 is addressed. Attacking P2 before P1 is a waste of energy.
OK — Healthy
Non-Constraint
Metrics graded OK are at or above benchmark. Protect these. Don't accidentally break what's working while fixing what isn't.

Step 3 — How to Use the Diagnosis

Getting the diagnosis is step one. Acting on it correctly is the whole point.
A
Identify Your P1 — and Only Work on P1
The most common mistake is reading the constraint diagnosis and then trying to fix everything at once. Don't. Fix your P1 first. If your P1 is hook rate, your entire month's creative energy goes into hook testing. If it's show rate, your entire focus is confirmation sequences and follow-up. One constraint. One fix. Then re-run the tracker.
B
Use Claude to Build a Fix Plan
After running the tracker, paste your results into Claude and ask it to build a specific action plan. With your data, Claude can give you precise, constraint-specific fixes — not generic advice.
My constraint diagnosis shows: - P1 Constraint: Show Rate (29% vs. 40% benchmark) - P2 Constraint: Hook Rate (18% vs. 25% benchmark) - Monthly ad spend: $3,200 - Offer price: $6,000 Based on this, give me a specific 30-day action plan to fix my show rate first, then my hook rate. Include the exact tactics, in what order, with what success metric I'm targeting for each.
C
Run It Monthly — Compare Period to Period
The tracker is most powerful as a monthly practice, not a one-time snapshot. Run it at the end of every month. Watch your P1 move from red to yellow to green over time. When your P1 resolves, your P2 becomes the new P1. That's how you compound growth — one constraint at a time.
