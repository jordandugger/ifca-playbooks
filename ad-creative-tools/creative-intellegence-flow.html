Part 1 — Do This Once · ~10 Minutes
Claude Project Setup
This creates your permanent creative OS. Every brief, every entity audit, every shoot plan lives here and builds on itself over time.
1
Create a Claude Pro account
Go to claude.ai and upgrade to Pro ($20/month). Required for Projects (persistent memory), Artifacts (the interactive tools), and the API the tools call internally.
claude.ai → Settings → Billing → Upgrade to Pro
2
Create a new Claude Project
In the left sidebar click Projects → New Project. Name it: [Your Name] — Ad Creative Intelligence. This is your permanent creative operating system — everything lives here and every conversation inherits the full context.
Left sidebar → Projects → New Project
3
Paste the Project Instructions — copy below
Click Project Instructions at the top of your project. Paste the full IFCA Creative Intelligence prompt. This loads the complete Andromeda framework, KPI rules, kill rules, entity ID logic, and your voice guidelines — so Claude operates as a trained strategist, not a generic assistant.

You are an embedded Meta ad creative strategist inside Impact Fitness Coaching Academy (IFCA). Your job is to help this client run a systematic, data-driven ad creative machine on Meta — using the Andromeda framework IFCA teaches at the Seven-Figure Retreat. You operate across two tools in sequence: 1. Swipe Brief Builder — convert intelligence reports into shoot-ready entity briefs 2. Weekly Creative Brief — produce a weekly shoot plan from the combined output Every conversation in this project is part of an ongoing creative production system...
This is the brain. Every conversation in this project inherits these instructions automatically — you never have to re-explain the framework.
4
Upload your three foundation files
Click Add Content in your project and upload these three files. This is what transforms Claude from a smart assistant into a strategist who knows your specific business — your numbers, your avatar, your market.
See the "What to Upload" reference below for exactly what each file does and why it matters.
5
Run Manus to generate your Intelligence Report
Open Manus and run a competitor research session using the IFCA co-work prompt from the AI Tools workshop. Manus will pull Meta Ad Library competitor data, scrape organic top performers, and map white space. Export the full report as a document, then upload it to your project.
Manus → run IFCA research prompt → export → upload to project
Re-run every 4–6 weeks or any time you hit a creative plateau. Upload the new report to update your project context.
6
Paste the Creative Intelligence Tool — copy below
Inside your Claude project, open a new conversation and paste the tool code below. Claude renders it as an interactive artifact. Step 1 (Swipe Brief Builder) and Step 2 (Weekly Creative Brief) both live inside the same tool — toggle between them. Paste your Manus intel report into Step 1 and generate your first entity briefs.

7
Map your current ad library in the Entity Audit Worksheet
Before running Step 2 of the tool, open the Entity Audit Worksheet and map every live ad by format, hook type, emotion, presenter, and funnel stage. This gives you your real entity count vs. your ad count — and confirms which brief entities fill actual gaps vs. ones you already have covered.

Part 2 — Reference Material
What to Upload + Pro Tips
📁 What to Upload (Step 4)
01
Data Constraints Tracker
Your business math — CPL targets, CAC, offer price, close rate, revenue equilibrium. Makes every brief financially calibrated to your actual numbers, not industry averages.
02
Copy Kit
Your avatar language, mechanism name, proof assets, and CTA keywords. Makes hooks sound like you — not generic fitness copy. The difference between a good brief and a great one.
03
Intelligence Report (from Manus)
Your market layer — competitor ads, organic top performers, white space map. Upload after each research session so Claude always has current market context.
⚡ Pro Tips
Re-upload your report monthly
Run a new Manus research session every 4–6 weeks. The market shifts. Fresh intel = better briefs with current white space.
Update KPI data regularly
Paste your latest hook rates and CPL into the project. Claude adjusts recommendations based on what's actually working right now.
Log winners and killed ads
After each kill or scale decision, tell Claude. It builds institutional memory — no more re-testing dead angles six months later.
One project per offer
If you run multiple offers, create separate projects. Each gets its own math, avatar context, and creative history. Don't mix them.
