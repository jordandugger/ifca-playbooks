Revenue Equilibrium

Revenue Equilibrium = LTV × New Clients acquired per month. This is the total lifetime value being added to the business each month. The Monthly Revenue Cap is what the business converges to if churn, close rate, and client value stay exactly flat — it's the ceiling you're building toward. If Equilibrium exceeds the Cap, the business is compounding. If it falls below, churn is winning even when you're closing.

Section 05
Constraint Diagnosis

Fix these in order. The top constraint is almost always structural — fixing #1 unlocks more value than fixing #2 through #5 combined.

1
No Paid Acquisition Engine — $2.35M Business Running on One Channel
The entire acquisition system is organic content → manual DM handling by the setter team. This model has a hard ceiling (setter bandwidth) and no ability to predictably increase call volume without increasing manual labor. At $197K/month, the business is mature enough that organic volatility (algorithm changes, content droughts, Vanessa's schedule) can create real revenue swings. A parallel paid channel is not optional — it's infrastructure.
Fix: Build the three-layer paid stack over the next 90 days. TOF = follower/engagement campaigns ($1–2 cost-per-follower target using best organic reels). MOF = DM lead magnet + inbound opt-in funnel (50/50 cold and warm retarget). BOF = short-form VSL sent directly to application. This is a $10–20K/month investment with a business that has $11.5K LTV per client. The math is very favorable.
2
Show Rate at 57% — Manual DM Funnel Is the Root Cause
Show rate is running at 57% against a 70% target, costing roughly $35–40K/month in lost sales opportunities. The root cause is not closer quality or offer price — it's that leads booked through manual DM outreach (even good organic traffic) show up at structurally lower rates than leads who opted into a structured funnel with confirmation sequences. Every manual DM booking is an unconfirmed prospect. An inbound funnel with GHL automation fixes this.
Fix: Build GHL inbound call booking funnel: opt-in → confirmation page with short video → application → calendar → automated pre-call sequence (3 touches before the call: SMS day-of, email day-before, SMS 2 hours before). Inbound prospects who self-select through a funnel consistently show at 70–80%+ vs. manually DM'd leads at 55–60%.
3
Calls Down 30/Month From Q4 — Omar Closing + Managing Simultaneously
The 30-call/month decline from Q4 to Q1 has two drivers: Q4 backend pipeline drain (normal) and Omar splitting his time between closing calls and managing/rebuilding the sales team through a turnover cycle. When the person responsible for closing is also responsible for hiring, training, and managing closers, close capacity drops. This is a structure problem, not a performance problem.
Fix: Define Omar's role clearly: is he the sales director (managing team, not closing) or the lead closer (closing, not managing)? At $197K/month revenue, the business can afford both. Hire a dedicated sales manager or promote from within so Omar can focus on one function exclusively. Meanwhile, the inbound funnel fix (Constraint 2) increases call volume without requiring additional setter headcount.
4
Media Buyer Accountability Gap — No Feedback Loop or Creative Brief System
Same media buyer as Alycia, but per Jordan's notes, the results "weren't quite into KPI" and "almost none of the calls were actually from these ads." This is consistent with a media buyer who is not receiving regular entity-specific creative briefs, KPI feedback, or structured testing protocols. The issue is not media buyer quality — it's that they're operating without a systematic brief-to-test-to-kill-or-scale workflow.
Fix: Implement a Weekly Creative Brief (30 minutes every Monday). Each brief specifies: 3 new entity concepts with hook, format, emotional driver, and CTA. Establish KPI review every Friday: hook rate, hold rate, cost-per-scheduled-call. Kill ads at 2× target CPL after 3 days of spend. Scale winners at 20% budget increases every 48 hours. The media buyer needs a system to execute against — not just "run some ads."
5
Facebook Channel Untapped — IG-Only Presence
At $197K/month revenue with a validated offer, there's no reason to be running on a single platform. Facebook represents a different and often older audience segment that overlaps with Vanessa's 30+ avatar — and Facebook cold traffic often converts better for premium coaching offers. The same creative assets that run on Instagram can be deployed to Facebook with minimal additional effort.
Fix: Once the IG paid stack is running and producing consistent leads, duplicate the campaigns to Facebook within 30 days. Budget allocation: 70% Instagram, 30% Facebook initially. Adjust based on CPL within the first month.
✓
Leave These Alone: LTV, Close Rate, AOV, FE → BE Conversion, Operating Margin
LTV at $11,568 is exceptional. Close rate at 34.4% is in the appropriately-priced range for a $4,489 offer. FE → BE conversion at 50.3% is at target. Operating margin at 45% is elite. Don't touch pricing strategy, close process, or backend offer structure — these are working. The only things to fix are acquisition volume and funnel infrastructure.
