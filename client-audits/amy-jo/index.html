Section 04 — Updated April 2026
Creative Intelligence Report

Full intelligence synthesis across brand positioning, offer pivot context, organic ecosystem, Meta Ad Library, competitive benchmarks, entity gap analysis, and verbatim hooks — ready for the Ad Creative Workshop entity-building exercise.

Amy Jo's creative system is currently good enough to acquire demand profitably — but not yet optimized to reflect the offer pivot or maximize downstream conversion quality. With a $1.47 CPL and 11.4× contracted ROAS, the existing ads are clearly working at the efficiency level. The structural problem is that the paid account spans two adjacent but distinct message realities at the same time: one built around body recomp for women over 40 (the legacy story), and one building toward hormone-aware metabolism and functional health testing (the emerging story). When these are blended without clear hierarchy, the market can understand the pain without fully understanding why Amy Jo's specific method is the right next step — which shows up as a soft lead-to-book rate in the DM funnel.

The highest-leverage creative move is not more ads in the same frustration-led anti-self-blame lane. The account already runs approximately 9 active ads with only ~4 distinct real entities, most of which share the same emotional driver (frustration) and the same narrative structure (you're not broken, you need a smarter strategy). The next creative cycle needs to add desire, identity aspiration, mechanism proof, and social proof entities that work with the DM-for-coaching funnel specifically — building lead quality, not just lead volume.

Section 05
Constraint Diagnosis
P1
FE→BE Conversion 30% — Most Valuable Unlockable Gap
The Q1 average FE→BE conversion of 30% sits 20 points below the 50% IFCA benchmark, and the success coach explicitly flags it as the primary constraint. January hit 30%, February 25%, March 35% — trending in the right direction but still significantly off target. At 8.3 closes per month with an average backend offer price of $3,230, closing the gap from 30% to 50% represents approximately 1.7 additional backend clients per month — roughly $5,500/month in additional contracted backend revenue at zero incremental ad spend. The success coach also notes that a poorly performing coach was recently fired, which was likely suppressing this number. With the new functional health coach onboarding, the natural ascension path — from recomp/metabolism frontend into hormone-aware functional health backend — becomes much cleaner.
Fix: (1) Define the explicit FE→BE ascension narrative: the frontend 12-week Blueprint addresses the body and metabolism; the backend takes the client into deeper functional health optimization, hormone testing interpretation, and ongoing protocol management. This is a natural continuation, not a separate pitch. (2) Build an AC resell coaching system: identify frontend clients approaching week 8–10 who are responding well, and initiate a structured backend conversation before the frontend program completes. (3) The new functional health coach creates a clear "what comes next" that didn't previously exist — use that as the backend offer narrative immediately.
P1
2.3% Lead-to-Book Rate — DM Funnel Under-Converting for Its Cost Level
With 3,586 Q1 leads and only 75 scheduled calls, the lead-to-book rate averages 2.3% (Jan 2.7%, Feb 1.3%, Mar 2.9%). For a DM-for-coaching funnel — where the CTA is "DM me directly for coaching" rather than a lead magnet opt-in — a 2.3% lead-to-book is softer than the funnel type warrants. The success coach confirms this is the second priority after FE→BE. The head of client success adds important context: Amy Jo is still transitioning from a follower funnel into a DM funnel, and the DM CTA is a direct coaching offer rather than a lead magnet — meaning the leads who respond are higher intent but the volume of responses is lower. The setter (Paulene) is managing this volume, but setter bandwidth and script calibration for the new offer messaging are the identified levers.
Fix: (1) Update the setter DM script to reflect the new functional health / Metabolic Hormone Blueprint positioning — the old recomp script may not be speaking to the new offer narrative clearly enough to convert the new audience segment. (2) Add a lead magnet option alongside the direct coaching CTA — a free resource (e.g., "The Hormone-Aware Metabolism Guide for Women 40+") creates a lower-friction first touchpoint that increases DM response volume before the setter pitch. (3) Use case study ads (Priority #2 in entity queue) to pre-qualify leads so that when they DM, they already understand the offer context — reducing the setter's cold-to-warm conversion burden.
P2
Offer Pivot Creative Gap — Paid Ads Behind Website Positioning
The website clearly positions the Metabolic Hormone Blueprint™ with hormone testing, functional labs, and a named mechanism. The paid account is still largely running recomp / anti-more-effort / anti-self-blame content that pre-dates the pivot. This is not causing a CPL problem yet (the existing ads still generate $1.47 CPL), but it is creating a message inconsistency between what the ad promises (smarter recomp for women 40+) and what the funnel delivers (functional health + hormone testing + Blueprint). That inconsistency makes the setter's job harder, can reduce close rate on the new offer, and means the highest-quality prospects for the functional health angle are not being specifically targeted or pre-qualified.
Fix: Build the "Metabolic Hormone Blueprint" front-door entity (Entity Build Queue Priority #1) and run it in parallel with the existing winner set. Do not pull the existing ads — they are still generating profitable demand. Add the new mechanism-led entities alongside them and let the algorithm discover which entity the pivot audience responds to. Over 60 days, the account will self-sort toward the ads that generate the highest-quality DM conversations for the new offer.
