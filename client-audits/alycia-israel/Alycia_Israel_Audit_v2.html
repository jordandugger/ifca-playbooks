Section 05
Constraint Diagnosis

Fix these in order. The top constraint is almost always structural — fixing #1 unlocks more value than fixing #2 through #5 combined.

1
DM Lead Magnet Ceiling — The Funnel Is Too Manual to Scale
The business hit $100K/month on 100% manual code — DMs from start to close, no automation, no inbound funnel architecture. That's impressive. It's also the ceiling. Calls are plateauing at 70–77/month regardless of how much ad spend increases. Increasing spend doesn't fix this — it just burns the setter team faster. The constraint is not creative, not budget, not sales ability. It's that every lead has to be personally DM'd through the entire sales cycle. You can't scale what you can't automate.
Fix: Build the MOF inbound funnel layer. GHL opt-in → video → application → calendar booking. Goal: 50% of bookings coming from inbound without manual DM follow-up within 90 days. This is not a replacement for DMs — it's the layer that runs parallel and absorbs volume the setter team can't handle manually.
2
No TOF / MOF / BOF Structure — All Budget Is One Layer
Virtually all spend is: TOF DM lead magnet → cold traffic → manual call → set → close. That's one funnel doing the work of three. It worked to $100K. It won't work to $200K without a complete architecture rebuild. The Masterclass is the first real MOF proof point — it's working and needs to be systematized. Bottom-of-funnel proof ads and direct-offer VSLs are completely missing from the visible library and from the current spend allocation. There's no retargeting stack. There's no warm audience monetization.
Fix: TOF = follower/engagement campaigns (engager audience building). MOF = lead magnet 50/50 cold + retargeting warm followers, plus GHL funnel. BOF = short-form VSL → application page → calendar. Each layer feeds the next. Budget allocation: TOF 20%, MOF 60–70%, BOF 15–20%.
3
FE → BE Conversion at 38% — $30K+/Month Left on the Table
Target is 50%+. At 38%, roughly 6 clients per month who should be converting to the $5K backend aren't. That's ~$30K in contracted backend revenue per month not captured — with zero additional ad spend required to capture it. Feb hit 50% and showed what the number looks like when the backend conversation is happening consistently. Jan was 17%. The variance itself is the signal: this isn't a pricing or offer problem — it's an inconsistent process problem.
Fix: Audit the backend conversion conversation. When does it happen? Who initiates? Is there a structured re-sign sequence in GHL? Is there a specific point in the client journey (week 8–10) where the conversation is proactively triggered? This should be systematized, not left to individual initiative.
4
Ad Creative Diversity — One Entity Running the Entire Paid Library
The visible Meta Ad Library shows one active creative entity: an authority-style podcast promo static graphic. This is almost certainly functioning as a MOF video views / nurture play — warming the follower and engager pool before the DM lead magnet converts them. That's a smart use of the format. The problem is it's the only entity running. There is no cold TOF hook-driven entity, no pain-led or identity-led talking head, no testimonial, no DM keyword CTA alongside it. One MOF nurture entity cannot carry a full acquisition funnel. More critically, the brand identity that makes Alycia distinctive ("The Functional Health Feminist") is completely absent from paid creative — it lives on her bio and in her organic content but has never been weaponized as a paid hook. The creative machine is running well below its capacity.
Fix: Keep the podcast entity if it's functioning as MOF video views retargeting — that's a legitimate warm audience build. What's missing is the cold TOF layer that feeds it, and the BOF direct-response layer that converts the audience it's warming. Target: 8–12 genuinely distinct entities at this spend level. Priority entity types to build first: (1) Cold TOF talking head with "Functional Health Feminist" identity hook — completely absent from paid, (2) Pain-led root-cause mechanism video for cold traffic — mechanism-specific hooks outperform broad symptom hooks in this niche, (3) Client transformation testimonial for BOF warm retargeting. Use the Weekly Creative Brief tool to build 2–3 distinct entity briefs every Monday. This is a 30-minute weekly process, not a production overhaul.
5
Labor Margin at 50%+ — Primary Net Profit Constraint
Labor is consuming half of every dollar collected. Target is 30–35%. This single constraint is suppressing net margin from a potential 45%+ to the current 24%. At $75K/month revenue, that's roughly $15–17K/month in additional profit being absorbed by delivery labor overhead. The business is growing — the labor model needs to scale with it, not ahead of it.
Fix: Map delivery labor per client. Identify what can be batched (group touchpoints instead of 1:1 for routine check-ins), delegated to junior staff, or replaced with GHL automation. The goal is not to cut quality — it's to stop scaling 1:1 labor linearly with client count.
6
GHL Automation Gaps — $100K/Month Built on Manual Infrastructure
The current system is manual throughout: manual DMs, manual nurture, manual booking confirmation, manual re-sign conversations. GHL is in place but significantly under-utilized for automation. The next $50K/month in revenue requires the manual work to be absorbed by systems — not by adding more human hours. The team is already at capacity on the manual side. Any further scale without automation means burning the team or accepting a hard revenue ceiling.
Fix: Prioritize three GHL automation builds: (1) Inbound booking funnel (opt-in → video → application → calendar), (2) Pre-call confirmation and show rate sequence, (3) Client journey re-sign trigger at weeks 8–10. Each of these directly impacts a metric in this document.
✓
CPL at $4.20 and Upfront Collection at 54% — Leave These Alone
CPL is well inside target. Upfront collection at 54% means the business is self-funding growth. Close rate at 32.7% (Q1 avg) is in the appropriately-priced range — the right response is to keep pushing front-end AOV up, not to chase a higher close rate by lowering price. Don't sacrifice these numbers in pursuit of the constraints above.
