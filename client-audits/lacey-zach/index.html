The Monthly Revenue Cap is what the business converges to if churn, close rate, and client value stay exactly flat — the ceiling you're building toward at the current pace. Verdict: Building — but the trend is fragile and churn is the multiplier compressing everything. Equilibrium ($127.8K) is 74% above the flat-state ceiling ($73.6K), which looks healthy on average. But the monthly trajectory tells the real story: January ran $245K equilibrium on 23 closes and 6.3% churn. The math is stark: at 13% churn with 17 new clients, the business is off-boarding ~16 clients while adding 17 — barely maintaining its base. The single highest-leverage input is churn, not acquisition. Every 2% reduction in monthly churn adds roughly $2,500–$4,000 to per-client LTV and pushes the equilibrium ceiling significantly higher on the same close volume.

The churn watch: The two veteran coach off-boardings in 2025 and five new coach hires over the last 9 months are the structural driver of the churn spike. New coaches mean new clients learning new delivery styles — inherently higher off-board risk in the first 90 days. As the coaching team stabilizes, churn should normalize toward the 6–7% range seen in January. If March's 13.2% persists into Q2, the active client base begins contracting despite solid close volume.

Section 05
Constraint Diagnosis
P1 — Critical
Show Rate Collapse: 57% vs 70% Target
Show rate degraded from the prior 67–70% range to 57.2% Q1 average (Jan 69.2% · Feb 51.6% · Mar 54.4%). The root cause is not setter effort — it's lead qualification. Lacey's viral surge added hundreds of cold, unvetted prospects who booked calls with no prior belief in the mechanism, no understanding of investment, and no commitment signal beyond clicking a DM CTA. These leads cancel, ghost, or no-show at dramatically higher rates than leads who have consumed MOF/BOF content before booking. At current show rate, 151 booked calls per quarter disappear before a pitch is even made.
Fix: Three-layer qualification stack — (1) AI DMer launches to pre-qualify via DM before any call booking is surfaced; (2) add friction to booking calendar: mandatory qualifier questions, explicit investment range disclosure, 24-hour cancellation policy with no rescheduling; (3) build MOF/BOF creative (HTMA reveal, case study, Zach entity) that pre-frames investment and effort requirements before leads hit the booking link. Target: 70%+ show rate by end of Q2.
P1 — Critical
Close Rate: 19.8% vs 30% Floor (Young Sales Team)
Q1 close rate averaged 19.8% (Jan 22.2% · Feb 21.5% · Mar 16.2%), the lowest reported since data tracking began. Two compounding factors: (1) the setter and closer team is largely new — 5 coaches hired in 9 months means the sales infrastructure is simultaneously onboarding new closers and selling to cold, unqualified traffic; (2) unqualified leads who do show up are harder to close because they have not consumed conviction content pre-call. Zach has been managing recruiting, training, hiring, and firing simultaneously — the feedback loop between calls and coaching has been compressed. The success coach notes April is already showing improvement (8 closes in first 9 days vs 11 for all of March).
Fix: (1) Weekly call review and feedback cadence — even 2 hours/week of structured call auditing will compress the learning curve on a young team; (2) provide closers with a pre-call brief showing which MOF/BOF content the prospect has engaged with, if trackable; (3) BOF creative that surfaces cost-of-inaction and social proof pre-call reduces the selling work required on the call itself. Target: 27–30% by Q3 as team matures and lead quality improves.
P1 — Critical
Churn Spike: 13.2% in March — New Coach Infrastructure Risk
Monthly churn spiked from 6.3% (January) to 13.2% (March) — more than double. The structural cause is the coaching team rebuild: two veteran coaches off-boarded clients in 2025, five new coaches were hired in 9 months, and the resulting handoff and onboarding process created elevated client dissatisfaction and off-boarding risk in the 60–90 day new-coach window. At 13% churn on 121 active clients, the business is losing 16 clients per month — nearly matching the 17 new clients being acquired. The compounding engine is essentially paused.
Fix: (1) 30/60/90-day structured client check-in protocol for all clients assigned to coaches hired in the last 9 months; (2) coaching quality scorecard — track client outcomes by coach to identify which new hires have the highest early off-board rates; (3) proactive retention intervention: identify at-risk clients at day 45 before they self-cancel. Target: churn back below 8% by Q2 end as new coaches stabilize their client load.
P2 — Meaningful Gap
DM Overflow: 500+ Unread Leads, No Scale Solution (Until AI Launch)
The manual setter system was built for a business generating leads at a prior pace. Since Lacey went viral in late Q3 2024, the minimum unread DM count has reportedly been 500+ for six months. The setter cannot surface the hottest leads, qualify at pace, or follow up systematically in an environment with that level of overflow. This is the single operational constraint that compounds every downstream metric: show rate, close rate, and lead-to-book ratio all suffer when qualification cannot happen at the speed the lead volume demands.
Fix: AI DMer launch is the solution — already in motion. Priority is speed of deployment and quality of qualifying questions in the DM flow. While the AI system launches, add more qualifying friction to the organic CTAs themselves (comment triggers should surface a 2-question qualifier sequence before surfacing the booking link, not immediately after the initial DM reply).
P2 — Meaningful Gap
Only 2 Active Paid Entities vs 8–10 Target
The Meta Ad Library shows 2 clearly distinct active paid entities — a mechanism-led educational comment trigger and a direct DM conversion play. Both are TOF/awareness-stage entities. There are no MOF entities building belief in the HTMA mechanism pre-call, no BOF entities deploying case-specific social proof, and no qualifying entities creating disqualification friction. With $12.6K/quarter in ad spend and a business generating 7.1:1 CAC:LTV, the creative system has enormous headroom to expand — but the additional entities need to address mid-funnel and bottom-funnel conversion gaps, not simply add more TOF volume to an already overloaded qualification system.
Fix: Build 6 net-new entities in this workshop, prioritizing the Zach dual-interview, HTMA lab reveal, symptom-to-result case study, whiteboard cortisol stack, and the qualification disqualifier. Add MOF retargeting budget — roughly 30% of total spend — to serve these entities to warm audiences who have already engaged with organic content.
P3 — Watch Item
January Overhead: 46.6% Margin (Annual Expenses)
January's overhead margin hit 46.6% ($32.2K on $67.9K revenue) — far above the 3–8% target — driven primarily by annual expenses and the costs associated with rebuilding the coaching infrastructure. This is a one-time event, not a structural problem. February (12.8%) and March (10.2%) are closer to normal operating range, though still above target. Q1 net margin averaged 36% because of January's noise; underlying operating efficiency (shown in Feb 52.1% and Mar 45.2%) is solid.
Fix: No immediate action required — January distortion normalizes in Q2. Watch March overhead (10.2%) for continued normalization. If overhead stays above 10% through Q2, investigate recurring software, subscription, or staffing costs that may have been added during the coaching team rebuild without a corresponding revenue increase.
OK — Leave Alone
CPL, ROAS, FE Price Point & CAC:LTV — All Elite
$3.53 CPL, 11.7× contracted ROAS, 6.6× collected ROAS, $3,882 avg FE price, and 7.1:1 CAC:LTV are all operating well above IFCA benchmarks. The ad machine itself is not broken. Marketing margin of 8.2% is slightly below the 10% floor but only because the business is underinvesting in ads relative to its LTV — not because the spend is inefficient. Do not pivot the creative strategy away from what's producing elite CPL. The expansion strategy is to add more creative diversity on top of the current winning foundation, not replace it.
