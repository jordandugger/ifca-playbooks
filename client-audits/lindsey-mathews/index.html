Section 05
Constraint Diagnosis
P1
DM Funnel Hitting Diminishing Returns — Narrow Entity Stack
The entire paid acquisition system runs through one funnel type (DM keyword) and one creative cluster (perimenopause frustration + eat less/move more contradiction). Q3 2025 was peak efficiency at $4K–$7K/mo spend. Q4 held but same deal count at double the spend. Q1 2026 — technically in KPI but trendline clearly weakening. Scheduled calls down 15% YoY. March showed clear deterioration on both booked-call rate and downstream CPA. 66 active ads, ~6–7 real entities. This is the constraint that owns everything else.
Fix
Build 8–10 truly distinct entities using the priority build queue (proof-led, identity, founder authority, editorial, inbound VSL). Retire duplicate variants that mask entity sameness. Introduce format diversity: testimonial video, text-led carousel, short VSL. Goal: 4 clearly different emotional/format clusters live simultaneously.
P1
No Inbound Call Funnel — Single Acquisition Path Risk
100% of paid acquisition runs through DM → setter → booked call. No opt-in funnel, no VSL landing page, no lead magnet with email sequence, no inbound direct call booking path. Pixel tracking issues historically prevented this. Per the success coach Loom, those issues appear resolved in Q1 2026. Operating with a single-path acquisition model at $38K+/month in spend creates fragility — any DM deliverability issue, setter performance dip, or algorithm shift creates outsized exposure.
Fix
Launch a parallel inbound funnel: lead magnet ad → opt-in page → email welcome sequence → call booking link. Even capturing 20–30% of acquisition through this channel adds diversification and builds a list. VSL-style creative for the landing page can also pre-qualify leads, improving booked-call quality without requiring more setter hours.
P2
Collected ROAS Below Target — Cash Efficiency Tightening
Q1 collected ROAS came in at 1.78x — below the 2x IFCA threshold. January was worst at 1.60x; March at 1.18x. Contracted ROAS is strong (5.34x), which means the offer and sales process are working — the cash efficiency gap is a collections and upfront payment issue. March's upfront cash collection rate dropped to 26.7%, dragging the collected ROAS down. February's 2.74x shows what the number should look like when collections are healthy.
Fix
Audit the payment structure and upfront offer mechanics. February's 34.5% upfront rate and 2.74x collected ROAS should be the floor, not the ceiling. Push upfront AOV incentives — bonus modules, enrollment bonuses, or pay-in-full discount framing. Target: 33%+ upfront rate consistently to bring collected ROAS above 2x.
P2
Labor Margin Running High — 54.5% vs 30–35% Target
Q1 labor margin is 54.5% of revenue — well above the 30–35% IFCA benchmark. Total labor expenses ran $153K on $281K in collected cash. This reflects the team of 7+ coaches plus setter/closer labor, which is inherent to the delivery model for a 228+ active client base. However, the margin structure leaves limited room for additional scaling spend without compressing net margin further. Net operating margin came in at 29.8% — below the 50% target.
Fix
The path to margin improvement runs through LTV and AOV, not cost-cutting. Each $500 increase in frontend AOV and 5% improvement in backend conversion adds ~$15K–$20K in monthly contracted revenue without additional labor cost. Prioritize LTV-maximizing moves (backend offer clarity, FE→BE conversion script work) over cost reduction on a lean coaching team.
P3
Show Rate at 69.1% — Just Below Benchmark
Q1 blended show rate of 69.1% is just under the 70% IFCA target, and January was notably weak at 62.6%. The success coach noted this is actually an improvement — 60 fewer scheduled calls this quarter than Q1 2025 but the same number of live calls, implying better show-up behavior. However, January's 62.6% suggests a meaningful number of lower-quality leads are still booking and not showing, which ties back to the lead qualification problem in the DM funnel.
Fix
Improving DM messaging quality upstream (longer, more specific copy that pre-qualifies intent) and adding a pre-call confirmation sequence (video, reminder, re-opt-in) can push show rate consistently above 70%. February's 73% is the right level. January's 62.6% is an outlier that should not be the baseline.
OK
Overall Close Rate — 37.9% and Sales Team Is Not the Bottleneck
Q1 overall close rate of 37.9% is within the 30–45% IFCA target range. February hit 46.6% (excellent). Sales team depth — 1.5–2 closers plus dedicated setters on both Facebook and Instagram — is solid and not capacity-constrained. The bottleneck is above sales: qualified lead volume and booking rate, not what happens once prospects are on the phone.
Maintain
No changes needed to the sales process or team structure. Focus any sales-layer investment on pre-call quality (better DM qualification, confirmation sequences, higher-intent lead magnet funnel) rather than close-rate mechanics. The closers are closing — they need better leads.
