Creating MicroLead Magnet Quizzes

How to build beautiful, interactive HTML quizzes with Claude in under 30 minutes — and host them free on GitHub as permanent traffic assets for your coaching business.

The Concept
Why Quizzes Beat PDFs Every Time

A PDF lead magnet is something people download and never open. A quiz is something people finish because they want to know their result. That difference — completion vs. abandonment — is the entire game in lead generation.

01
It Feels Personal
They get their result — not generic info. The moment someone reads "The Stressed & Depleted Type" and thinks "that's literally me," you've built more trust than a 10-page PDF ever could. Personalization = perceived value.
02
It Creates a Micro-Commitment
Someone who answers 5 questions has invested 2 minutes. They want the payoff. Completion rates on well-designed quizzes run 70–85% — versus 20–30% for a PDF that requires a download and an open. More completions = more people who see your CTA.
03
The Result Page Does Your Selling
After someone gets their personalized result, they're in the most receptive state possible. That's where your booking link lives. It doesn't feel like a pitch — it feels like a natural next step. This is the highest-leverage placement for any CTA in your marketing.
04
It's Free to Host Forever
No Kajabi. No Squarespace. No monthly fees. One HTML file on GitHub Pages = a live URL you can run ads to, put in your bio, send in emails, and DM to leads — permanently, for free. This is the new standard for creating resources.
Step 1 — Choose Your Concept
Proven Quiz Ideas for Health & Fitness Coaches

The best quiz titles sound like something your ideal client Googles at 11pm. They're specific, pain-driven, and hint at a surprising answer. Here are five that convert:

Why Are You Always Tired?
Universal pain. Every woman 30–55 relates. Routes to 3 hormone types. Best for: functional health coaches
What's Your #1 Gut Health Saboteur?
Specific and searchable. Strong for coaches working with IBS, bloating, and inflammation. Best for: gut health coaches
What's Killing Your Metabolism?
Contrarian hook — implies their effort isn't the problem, their approach is. Best for: weight loss coaches
What's Your Hormone Type?
Identity-based result. Highly shareable. Works for broad female wellness audiences. Best for: women's health coaches
Is Your Diet Actually Working for Your Body?
Challenges existing behavior without blame. Opens the door to a new approach. Best for: nutrition coaches
Why Can't I Lose Weight? (It's Not What You Think)
Curiosity + contrarian hook. The parenthetical creates a pattern interrupt that stops the scroll. Best for: fitness coaches
The Rule
"Your quiz title should sound like something your ideal client would Google at 11pm when they're frustrated and can't sleep."
Step 2 — Write Your Claude Prompt
The Exact Prompt Template

This is the entire prompt. Copy it, swap in your details, paste it into Claude. You'll have a working first draft in under 2 minutes. The yellow text is what you customize.

Claude Prompt — Copy & Customize
I'm a [your specialty] coach who helps [your target client] achieve [your core result]. I want to build a free interactive HTML quiz called "[Your Quiz Title] — The 2-Minute Quiz" to use as a lead magnet. Here's how it should work: The quiz asks 5 simple multiple choice questions about their symptoms, habits, and lifestyle. Based on their answers, it diagnoses one of three types: 1. [Type 1 Name] — [brief description] 2. [Type 2 Name] — [brief description] 3. [Type 3 Name] — [brief description] Each result page should: - Name their type with a bold, empowering headline - Give 3–4 sentences explaining what's actually going on (science-adjacent, not clinical) - Include one specific action they can take this week - Have a CTA button that says "Book Your Free [Audit/Clarity/Strategy] Call" linking to: [YOUR BOOKING LINK] - Feel warm, empowering, and personalized — not like a generic blog post Design requirements: - Single self-contained HTML file (no external files needed) - Clean, modern design using [your brand colors or aesthetic — e.g. warm creams, sage green, terracotta] - Mobile friendly with a progress bar - Smooth animated transitions between questions - No data collection — purely informational Build the complete working HTML file.
💡
Pro Tip: The 3-Type Structure
Always build 3 result types — not 2, not 5. Two feels binary and unsophisticated. Five is too many to write compelling copy for. Three gives people a result that feels specific enough to be accurate without being so niche it alienates half your audience.

Step 4 — Host It Free on GitHub
Live on the Internet in 5 Minutes

GitHub Pages hosts static HTML files for free, forever. No server. No monthly fee. Here's the exact process:

1
Create a Free GitHub Account
Go to github.com → Sign up → Use your Gmail or any email. It's completely free. No credit card.
✓ One-time setup
2
Create a New Repository
Click the green New button → Name it something clean like hormone-quiz or metabolism-quiz → Hit Create Repository.
3
Add Your HTML File
Click Add File → Create New File → Name it exactly index.html → Paste your entire HTML code from Claude → Click Commit Changes.
⚠️ The filename must be index.html — not quiz.html or anything else. GitHub Pages looks for this exact name.
4
Enable GitHub Pages
Go to Settings → Pages → Under Source, select Deploy from a branch → Select main branch → / (root) → Save.
5
Get Your Live URL
Wait 60 seconds and refresh the Pages settings. GitHub gives you a live URL at:
https://yourusername.github.io/hormone-quiz
✓ Share anywhere — free forever

Quality Check
What Makes a Great Quiz vs. a Bad One

Most quiz lead magnets fail for the same predictable reasons. Here's the exact checklist to make sure yours doesn't.

✓ Great Quiz
✅5 questions maximum — people finish it
✅Multiple choice only — zero friction
✅Result feels personally accurate
✅One clear CTA on the result page
✅Title is a felt pain or curiosity hook
✅3 result types — specific but inclusive
✅Result includes one action to take this week
✗ Bad Quiz
❌15+ questions — people abandon it
❌Open text fields — too much effort
❌Generic result anyone could get
❌Three CTAs competing for attention
❌Title is generic ("Health Quiz")
❌2 types (binary) or 6+ types (overwhelming)
❌Result just ends with no next step

Step 6 — Connect to Go High Level
Wire Every Quiz CTA Back Into GHL

Your quiz lives on GitHub Pages, but your leads need to flow back into your GHL pipeline. Here are the three ways to wire it — from simplest to most automated.

A
Option 1 — Direct Calendar Link (Simplest)
In your Claude prompt, the CTA button links directly to your GHL booking calendar URL (e.g. go.yoursite.com/call). When someone clicks "Book Your Free Call," they land on your GHL calendar and book directly. GHL creates the contact automatically on booking. Zero extra setup required — this is the default and what the live demo uses.
B
Option 2 — GHL Opt-In Form Embed (Captures Email Before Booking)
Instead of linking directly to the calendar, link to a GHL landing page with an opt-in form. The form captures name + email first, adds them to a pipeline or list, triggers your email nurture sequence, then redirects to the booking calendar. Best for building your list in parallel with booking calls.

How to set up: In GHL, create a simple funnel step with just a name + email form. Set the redirect URL to your calendar. Use that funnel URL as your quiz CTA link.
C
Option 3 — Result-Specific GHL Pipelines (Most Powerful)
Build a different GHL funnel URL for each result type. Each result page CTA links to a different opt-in page with messaging tailored to that type — e.g. the "Stressed & Depleted" result links to a page that says "We have a specific protocol for your type." GHL tags the contact with their result type automatically based on which funnel they came through.

Why this is powerful: Your closers know the lead's result type before they dial. They can reference it on the call: "I saw you identified as the Stressed & Depleted type — that's actually the most common one we see, and it has the clearest fix." Instant rapport, faster close.
Claude Prompt — Add Result-Specific GHL Links
The quiz is built. Now I want each result page to link to a different booking URL depending on which result the person gets. Result 1 ([Type 1 Name]) → CTA links to: [GHL Funnel URL 1] Result 2 ([Type 2 Name]) → CTA links to: [GHL Funnel URL 2] Result 3 ([Type 3 Name]) → CTA links to: [GHL Funnel URL 3] Update the HTML so each result page CTA button points to the correct URL for that result type. Keep everything else exactly the same.
GHL Integration Rule
"Don't paste raw HTML into GHL's page builder — it strips the JavaScript and the interactivity breaks. The right move is host on GitHub Pages, then use GHL for the funnel pages the CTA links to. Best of both worlds: beautiful interactive quiz + full GHL pipeline automation."

Do It Right — Inside a Claude Project
Build Your Quiz Inside a Claude Project

Building your quiz inside a Claude Project instead of a regular chat gives you persistent context — Claude remembers your brand, niche, GHL links, and color palette across every session. You never re-explain yourself.

1
Create a New Project in Claude
Go to Claude.ai → Projects → New Project. Name it something like "Lead Magnet Quizzes" or "[Your Brand] — Marketing Assets."
✓ Takes 30 seconds
2
Set Your Project Instructions
Click Edit Project Instructions and paste in your brand context so Claude knows your niche, voice, and offer without you explaining it every time.
You are helping me build marketing assets for my coaching business. My niche: [your specialty and target client] My core offer: [offer name + price + what they get] My GHL booking link: [your booking URL] My brand colors: [hex codes or color descriptions] My tone: [e.g. warm, direct, science-backed, empowering] When I ask you to build something, apply this context automatically.
3
Upload Your Knowledge Files
Add files to the Project that Claude can reference — your brand voice doc, testimonials, your mechanism or methodology doc, and any past content. Claude uses these to write in your exact voice.
✓ Do this once — leverage forever
4
Run the Quiz Prompt Inside the Project
Now paste the master prompt from Section 3. Because your GHL link, brand colors, and niche are already in the Project, you only need to fill in the quiz title and result types — Claude fills the rest automatically.
✓ First draft in under 2 minutes
5
Iterate and Build Your Second Quiz in the Same Project
Your second quiz takes 10 minutes because Claude already has all your context. Just say: "Build me another quiz for [new topic]. Same brand style, same GHL link." Claude does the rest. Build 3 quizzes = 3 permanent cold traffic entry points into your funnel.
