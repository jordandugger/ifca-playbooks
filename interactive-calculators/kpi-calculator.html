Step 2 — Enter Your Inputs

Two required inputs. Everything else auto-fills.
1
Enter Your Offer Price
This is the front-end price of your primary coaching offer — the thing you're selling on calls. If you have multiple offers, use the one you're actively running ads for. Every KPI threshold recalculates instantly when this changes.
💡
If you offer payment plans, use the full value (e.g., 3 × $2,000 = enter $6,000). Your CAC ceiling is based on full contract value, not the first payment.
2
Enter Your Current Close Rate
Your actual close rate from the past 30–90 days. Don't use your best month — use your average. This drives your CPL target and entity count recommendation.

In the "Your Numbers vs. Targets" section, enter your actual hook rate, CPL, and hold rate from Meta Ads Manager. The calculator grades each metric green/yellow/red against your personalized benchmarks. This is the fastest way to see exactly where you're at vs. where you need to be.

Step 3 — How to Apply Your KPI Framework

The calculator gives you numbers. Here's how to use them to actually manage your ad account.
A
Use Kill Rules Religiously
Print your kill rule thresholds and put them somewhere visible. The rule: if a new ad spends 2× your target CPL without booking a call, kill it. If a previously-winning ad spends 3× your target CPL in a week without results, kill it. Not reduce it. Kill it. The algorithm has made its decision.
Ad TypeKill ThresholdAction
New entity (first 7 days)Spend 2× target CPL with 0 qualified callsKill. Archive. Analyze the hook, not the audience.
Existing winnerSpend 3× target CPL in a 7-day window decliningKill. Build a variation — don't patch the original.
B
Use Creative Cadence as Your Production Schedule
The calculator outputs exactly how many new entities to launch per week based on your spend tier. That number is your creative team's weekly minimum. It's not aspirational — it's the volume needed to continuously replenish your entity library as winners fatigue and losers get killed.
⚠️
Most coaches at $1,500/month in ad spend need 2–3 new entities per week to maintain a healthy library. If you're only launching 1–2 per month, you're running out of creative inventory faster than you're replenishing it.
C
Update It When Your Offer Price Changes
If you raise (or lower) your offer price, re-open the calculator and update. Your entire KPI framework shifts. A 2× increase in offer price roughly doubles your CAC tolerance — which means your CPL target, kill thresholds, and budget allocation all change. The calculator recalculates everything in real time.
