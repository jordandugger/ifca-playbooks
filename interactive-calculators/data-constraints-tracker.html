Two ways to get your data in: Option A — paste your Google Sheet URL and this tool pulls your latest numbers automatically every time you open it. Option B — export a CSV from your sheet and drop it in. Either way, you enter data once in your existing tracker and never type it here.

Option A — Google Sheets Live Connection
Recommended · One-time setup · Automatic from then on
1
Open Your Data Constraints Tracker in Google Sheets
If you're still on Excel, go to File → Save a copy to Google Drive first. You need the file to be in Google Sheets for this to work.
2
Publish Your Sheet to the Web
In Google Sheets, go to File → Share → Publish to web
In the dialog:
• Under Link, select the 2026 sheet tab (not the whole document)
• Change the format dropdown from Web page to Comma-separated values (.csv)
• Click Publish → confirm with OK
Copy the URL it gives you — it will look like this:
https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/[YOUR_SHEET_ID]/pub?gid=0&single=true&output=csv
3
Paste Your Published Sheet URL Here
Paste the CSV URL from Step 2 and click Load. This tool will pull your latest numbers every time it opens.
✓ Sheet connected. Numbers loaded into the Inputs tab. Recalculate to refresh your diagnosis.
4
Every Month Going Forward
Just update your Google Sheet as normal. When you open this tool, it auto-fetches the latest data. Hit Calculate and your diagnosis updates. No manual entry here required.
Option B — CSV Upload
No internet required · Works with Excel or Google Sheets
1
Export Your Sheet as CSV
Google Sheets: File → Download → Comma-separated values (.csv) — make sure you're on the 2026 tab first.
Excel: File → Save As → CSV (Comma delimited)
2
Drop the CSV File Here
📂
Click to browse or drag & drop your CSV
Exports from Google Sheets or Excel both work
✓ CSV loaded. Numbers mapped to the Inputs tab. Hit Calculate to run your diagnosis.
How This Tool Reads Your Sheet
This tool looks for specific row labels in column A of your 2026 sheet and pulls the YTD or most recent month value. As long as your sheet uses the standard IFCA Data Constraints template, this works automatically.

Important Note on CORS

Google Sheets published URLs sometimes block direct browser fetch requests due to CORS restrictions. If the auto-load fails, use Option B (CSV export/upload) instead — it works identically and takes under 60 seconds. The CSV route is actually more reliable for monthly use.
