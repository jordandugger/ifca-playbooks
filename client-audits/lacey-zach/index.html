SECTION 04
CONSTRAINT DIAGNOSIS
ONE-LINE DIAGNOSIS: Lacey & Zach's business is not broken at the lead or ROAS level — the revenue ceiling lives in two places simultaneously: a show rate 13 points below target that is burning booked calls before they happen, and a close rate 10+ points below target that is leaving roughly $30K in contracted revenue per month on the table.
P1 — CRITICAL SHOW RATE AT 57% — THE LEAD QUALITY / SHOW-UP SYSTEM IS BROKEN
Q1 2026 show rate of 57.2% against a 70%+ target means that roughly 13 of every 100 booked calls are evaporating before they happen. With 353 calls scheduled in Q1 and only 202 live calls, that's 151 calls that were booked and never showed — at an average FE close of $3,882, that's potentially $116K+ in contracted revenue that never had the chance to exist. January performed best at 69.2% but February dropped to 51.6% — the inconsistency suggests the problem isn't the lead source, it's the post-booking nurture and confirmation system. This is a show rate problem, not a traffic problem.
FIX → Implement a 3-touch post-booking confirmation sequence: (1) immediate confirmation with educational value (a 5-min Lacey video on what to expect on the call + why most women don't get the answers they need until now), (2) 24-hour reminder with a "quick question" text to confirm commitment, (3) 2-hour reminder with the specific call link. Layer in: a short pre-call assessment form that creates investment and filters low-intent leads before they waste setter time. Target: 65% by end of Q2, 72% by end of Q3.
P1 — CRITICAL CLOSE RATE AT 19.8% — 10+ POINTS BELOW MINIMUM VIABLE TARGET
A Q1 close rate of 19.8% (dropping to 16.2% in March) against a 30%+ target represents the largest single revenue lever in this business. If Lacey closed 30% of the same 202 live calls in Q1 instead of 19.8%, that's 20 additional clients at $3,882 each — $77,640 in additional Q1 contracted revenue on zero additional ad spend. The cancellation data provides some signal: three of the six Q1 cancellations involved client misunderstandings about what the program included (Stevi Scott assumed labs were included; Trish Nebo said the coach wasn't responsive). This suggests close-side qualification gaps — clients are being closed without a clear picture of what's included, creating buyer's remorse that costs money on both ends.
FIX → Three interventions: (1) Review every Q1 call recording — identify the top 3 objection patterns where deals are dying (price, time commitment, trust, clarity on deliverables). (2) Build a pre-call primer video that sets crystal-clear expectations about lab costs, what's included, and what Phase 1 looks like before the prospect ever gets on the call — this prevents close-side surprises and filters uncommitted leads. (3) Standardize a closer script that explicitly walks through the HTMA report card reveal as the demo during the call — prospects who see a sample lab result on the call close at higher rates because the mechanism becomes tangible. Target: 25% by end of Q2.
P2 — MEANINGFUL GAP AD ENTITY DIVERSITY — 3–4 REAL ENTITIES RUNNING WHERE 8–10 ARE NEEDED
The CPL data ($3.53 Q1 average) confirms that the current creative is performing well, but the narrow format range — primarily DM automation talking-head and static ads — is limiting the algorithm's ability to find buyers across different audience segments and creative exposure states. Lacey's organic library contains at minimum four distinct proven format concepts (I WOULD NEVER product callout, whiteboard symptom teaching, kitchen counter conditional, and the origin story personal narrative) that have never been converted to paid entities. The Manus audit confirms none of Lacey's competitors are running these formats in paid — meaning these are uncontested audience segments with zero creative saturation.
FIX → Immediate entity build queue: prioritize the I WOULD NEVER toxic product callout (TOF), the whiteboard cortisol symptom stack (TOF/MOF), and the Zach co-presenter empathy angle (MOF) as the next three shoots. Each requires a distinct CTA keyword, distinct format, and a genuinely different emotional driver to qualify as a new Andromeda entity. Target: 8 active diverse entities by end of Q2 — maintain 70/30 budget split between proven winners and testing entities.
P2 — MEANINGFUL GAP JANUARY OVERHEAD SPIKE — $32K IN OVERHEAD ON $69K COLLECTED REVENUE
January's overhead expenses of $32,229 (46.6% overhead margin against a 3–8% target) destroyed what should have been a $33K+ profit month and delivered only $1,778 in net operating income. February and March normalized to $11–12K overhead, suggesting January contained a non-recurring event (possibly a large equipment purchase, event cost, legal fee, or other one-time item). Without knowing the specific source, this cannot be categorized definitively — but it must be identified and either explained as non-recurring or controlled as a systemic cost.
FIX → Categorize every January overhead line item. If non-recurring: note it, move on, and use Feb–Mar as the true operating baseline. If systemic: build a monthly overhead budget cap with approval required above $12K. The Q1 net margin of 36% should normalize to 45–50%+ once overhead is controlled — this is the difference between a $30K/month profit business and a $45–50K/month profit business at current revenue.
P3 — WATCH FE→BE CONVERSION VOLATILITY — 36% TO 66% IN CONSECUTIVE MONTHS
A 30-point swing in FE→BE conversion between January (36%) and February (66%) with no clear operational explanation suggests the backend offer is being presented reactively rather than systematically. When the offer presentation is reliant on individual coach timing and framing, you get variance. The target is 50%+ consistently — the business reached 66% in February which proves the ceiling exists, the question is why it collapsed to 43% in March. A systematic backend offer presentation at weeks 8–10 of Phase 1 (when first big wins are documented) is the standard fix.
FIX → Build a Week 8 "Results Review + Backend Presentation" call protocol. Every Phase 1 client receives this call at a defined point in the program. The call documents their transformation to date using the metric framework from Phase 1, then presents the Phase 2 continuation as the natural next step. Take the backend offer presentation out of coach discretion and put it into the delivery system as a mandatory milestone.
✓ — DO NOT TOUCH TOP-OF-FUNNEL AD PERFORMANCE — DON'T CHANGE WHAT'S WINNING
The CPL machine is running perfectly. $3.53 CPL against a $10 target is genuinely best-in-class for this niche. The current winning entities are doing their job. The directive is: keep the budget on existing winners, run new entity tests at 30% of budget only, do not restructure the campaign, and do not change the targeting on proven performers. The problem is downstream — show rate and close rate. Do not attempt to fix the top of funnel in response to a mid/bottom funnel constraint.
PROTECT → Maintain 70/30 budget split (proven winners / new entity tests). Apply 3x KPI kill threshold to existing winners — do not kill what's working on gut feel. Any new entity tested receives the 2x KPI threshold before a kill decision is made. The funnel is filling correctly. Fix the downstream conversion, not the source.
