Manus Task #1 — Instagram Profile Audit

Step 01 — Manus Prompt

Before touching the Ad Library: Always audit the Instagram profile first. You need to understand WHO they're speaking to and WHAT emotional territory they own before you can identify gaps. This context shapes everything that follows.

📋 COPY-PASTE INTO MANUS
REPLACE YELLOW TEXT
Go to Instagram.com/[COMPETITOR_HANDLE] and analyze their profile. Tell me:

1. AVATAR — Who exactly are they speaking to? Be specific: gender, business stage, 
   niche, income level, pain point they hammer most.

2. CORE MECHANISM — What do they claim makes their approach unique or different?

3. DOMINANT CONTENT FORMAT — What shows up most? (talking head rants, b-roll lifestyle, 
   educational carousels, skits, client results)

4. TOP 3 POSTS (last 60 days) — For each: note the hook/topic and why it likely 
   performed well.

5. EMOTIONAL TERRITORY — What emotions dominate their content? 
   (aspiration, frustration, humor, fear, authority)

6. PROFILE CONVERSION AUDIT — If someone saw their ad and visited this profile, 
   would they trust and want to buy? Why or why not? Be honest.

Output as a structured report with clear headers for each section.
WHAT YOU GET BACK
Avatar definition  ·  Core mechanism  ·  Content format breakdown  ·  Top post hooks  ·  Emotional tone map  ·  Profile conversion assessment
WHAT TO DO WITH IT
Paste into Claude alongside your own profile analysis. Ask: "What emotional territory are they owning that I'm not? Where's the overlap and where's the gap?"
IFCA • AI Tools Workshop — Module 017

Manus Task #2 — Ad Library Deep Dive

Step 01 — Manus Prompt
📋 COPY-PASTE INTO MANUS — AD LIBRARY RESEARCH
REPLACE YELLOW TEXT
Go to: [COMPETITOR AD LIBRARY URL]

Filter to active ads only. For every ad that has been running 30+ days (these are 
spending money = working), pull the following for each ad:

1. HOOK (VERBATIM) — Write the first 3 seconds word-for-word. If it's a visual 
   hook with no spoken word, describe exactly what's on screen.
2. BANNER TEXT — What does the text overlay at the top of the video say?
3. FORMAT BUCKET — talking head / b-roll+text / skit / testimonial / 
   VSL-style / interview / static image
4. EMOTIONAL DRIVER — frustration / desire / fear / humor / identity / social proof
5. CTA KEYWORD — What word or action do they use? (DM, comment keyword, link click)
6. RUN DURATION — How long has this ad been active?

After pulling all ads, give me:
- TOTAL active ads running
- REAL entity count (same format + same emotion = same entity, not two)
- FORMAT GAPS — which buckets are they NOT covering?
- ENTITY DIVERSITY VERDICT — are they running a real creative machine or 
  just variations of the same thing?
- TOP 3 HOOKS I SHOULD STEAL — their longest-running, likely highest-performing. 
  Format: Hook (verbatim) | Why it works | Format bucket | Emotion
30+ DAY RULE
Ads running 30+ days are actively spending budget. That means they're working. These are the ones worth studying.
LEGAL NOTE
The Meta Ad Library is public by law. Reading it carries zero risk of account shutdown. This is 100% within Meta's terms of service.
TIME ESTIMATE
Manus takes 15–30 minutes per competitor. Let it run in background while you work on something else.

Step 3 — Feed Everything Into Claude

The Synthesis Prompt
THE BRIDGE STEP
This is where intelligence becomes creative output. You're feeding Claude two things simultaneously — what your competitors are doing AND what's already working for you — and asking it to find the intersection.
WHAT YOU PASTE IN
1
Manus intelligence report
2
Your top 5 ad scripts (last 90 days)
3
Your best organic posts from Foreplay
WHAT COMES OUT
10–60 pre-differentiated ad scripts  ·  White space analysis  ·  Creative brief for next shoot day  ·  Entity diversity map
📋 CLAUDE SYNTHESIS PROMPT — COPY-PASTE READY
Here is a competitor intelligence report from Manus:
[PASTE MANUS OUTPUT]

Here are my own top-performing ad scripts and organic posts 
from the last 90 days:
[PASTE YOUR TOP ADS + ORGANIC POSTS]

Do the following:

1. Identify every hook formula being used across both sets — 
   group by type (pain callout / result lead / curiosity / 
   identity / pattern interrupt)

2. List which FORMAT BUCKETS are most common vs. underused 
   across competitor content

3. Find the WHITE SPACE — angles nobody is covering that match 
   what my avatar actually cares about

4. Generate 10 new ad scripts, each targeting a different hook, 
   format, and angle. No two should be in the same creative 
   cluster (different Entity ID)

5. For each script, label:
   Format Bucket | Hook Type | Emotion | CTA Keyword | 
   Why this is a new entity

Output the scripts in order from most to least 
contrarian/unexpected.
