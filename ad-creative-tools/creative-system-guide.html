Ad File Naming Convention
Why Naming Matters

The tool generates a unique file name for every entity. Use it for three things: your exported video file, your ad name in Meta Ads Manager, and your performance tracking sheet. When results come back, you can match every data point to the exact creative entity that produced it.

📁
The Naming Convention
Auto-generated by the tool — copy it off every shoot card
[BRAND]_[FUNNEL]_[FORMAT]_[EMOTION]_[PRESENTER]_[ConceptSlug]_v[N]
IFCA_TOF_TH_FRUST_COACH_HormoneBreaker_v1
SegmentWhat it meansExamples
BRANDYour brand abbreviated, max 8 charsIFCA · TRAINER · SSHIFTS · MOXIE
FUNNELFunnel stageTOF · MOF · BOF
FORMATFormat bucket abbreviationTH · BR · SK · VSL · INT · WLK · UGC · CAR
EMOTIONCore emotion abbreviationFRUST · DESIRE · FEAR · HOPE · ASPIR · PRIDE · CURIO · SKEPT
PRESENTERWho delivers the adCOACH · CLIENT · PART · TEAM · UGC · VO
ConceptSlugParent concept, PascalCase, max 3 wordsHormoneBreaker · DoctorDismissal · KimStory · SummerConfidence
v[N]Version number — increments on hook swapsv1=original · v2=hook swap · v3=next hook swap
Hook swap rule: If you test a different hook on the same entity (same format + emotion + presenter), increment the version: v1 → v2 → v3. This keeps the entity fingerprint intact in your tracking while distinguishing which hook you tested. A different format, emotion, or presenter = new entity name entirely.
In Meta Ads Manager: When you upload the video, set the Ad Name to the exact file name. When you pull results later — hook rate, hold rate, CPL, ROAS — you can sort by Ad Name and instantly see which entity, which funnel stage, which emotion, and which version produced each result. No more guessing which "Oct talking head" was the winner.
Creative Volume by Spend Tier
How Many Entities Per Week

The Weekly Creative Brief automatically calculates how many entities to schedule based on your spend level. Here's the full breakdown — and what your weekly production rhythm should look like at each tier.

$500–$1,500/month
2–3 entities/week
1 shoot day per week. Prioritize hook tests on your single strongest concept. Focus on getting one format right before diversifying. Batch 2–3 hook variants in one session.
$1,500–$5,000/month
3–5 entities/week
1–2 shoot days per week. Start mixing format types — one talking head day, one B-roll or UGC day. Prioritize filling the biggest format gap each week. Run at least one BOF ad at all times.
$5,000–$10,000/month
5–8 entities/week
2 shoot days per week. Dedicate one day to talking head / founder content, one day to client testimonials / UGC. Refresh intel report every 4–6 weeks. Maintain 70/30 budget split: 70% on proven winners, 30% on new entity tests.
$10,000+/month
8–12 entities/week
3 shoot days per week. Full creative team or dedicated UGC operator. Monthly entity audit mandatory. Run parallel concept lines — at least 3 active concepts with 3+ entities each. Re-run Manus monthly.
Kill rules: New ad — spend 2× your CPL target before killing. Existing winner — spend 3× target before killing. Never edit a live ad — duplicate → modify → launch as new. Editing resets micro-signal learning.
Keep the System Sharp
What to Re-Upload and When

Your Claude Project compounds over time — but only if you keep the data fresh. Here's exactly what to update and how often.

File Frequency When to update / what triggers a re-upload
Manus Intelligence Report Every 4–6 weeks Competitor landscape shifts. New ad formats appearing in your niche. Hook rates dropping across the board (market saturation signal). Hit a creative plateau.
Ad Performance History Monthly After each monthly review, export your Meta Ads Manager results (ad name, hook rate, hold rate, CPL, status) and re-upload. Claude builds a kill/scale memory that prevents re-testing dead angles.
Data Constraints Tracker Quarterly or on change When your offer price changes. When you hit a new spend tier. When your CAC target shifts after a pricing or delivery change. When close rate changes materially (±5%).
Copy Kit Quarterly or on change When you rebrand or rename your mechanism. When you add new proof assets (big client wins, revenue milestones). When your avatar language shifts after market research.
KPI Snapshot (inline) Every brief session Each time you run the tool, paste your current hook rate, hold rate, and CPL into Tab 3's input. You don't re-upload this — you paste it fresh every time so Claude has current data for the shoot plan.
How to re-upload: Open your Claude Project → click the file in Project Files → delete it → click Add Content → upload the new version. Claude automatically uses the latest version in all future conversations.
