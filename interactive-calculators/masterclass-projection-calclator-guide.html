Step 3 — How to Use It Across Campaigns

The projection calculator is a decision tool, not just a math tool. Here's the three-part workflow.
1
Never Launch Without a Projected ROAS ≥ 3×
If your model shows projected ROAS under 3×, don't launch — fix the inputs. Most of the time the fix is one of these: increase offer price, improve your close rate assumption, or shrink the campaign size to a lower-spend test. Don't talk yourself into launching a campaign that doesn't pencil out on paper.
The 3× Rule
Campaign #1 minimum: 3× ROAS. Do not scale budget past Campaign #1 spend without hitting this threshold. The projection calculator exists to enforce this rule before you spend — not to explain a bad result after.
2
Use Scenario Planning to Find Your Highest-Leverage Lever
The most powerful use of the calculator: change one variable at a time and see how much it moves ROAS. Which single improvement — +10% show rate, +5% close rate, or -$5 CPL — has the biggest ROAS impact? That's where your energy goes between campaigns.
My last masterclass results: - Ad spend: $1,500 | CPL: $22 | LP conversion: 31% - Show rate: 24% | Retention: 78% | Offer→book: 14% | Close rate: 38% - Offer price: $5,500 | Actual ROAS: 2.1× I'm below my 3× ROAS target. Run three scenarios for my next campaign: Scenario 1: I only improve show rate to 35% (everything else same) Scenario 2: I only improve close rate to 48% (everything else same) Scenario 3: I only reduce CPL to $15 (everything else same) Which scenario produces the highest ROAS and which lever should I focus on first?
3
Log Every Campaign to History
The calculator includes a campaign history log. After each masterclass, save the inputs and results. After 3 campaigns you'll have enough data to see real trends — which KPIs are improving, which are stagnant, and whether your ROAS trajectory is moving in the right direction. This is what separates coaches who systemize their masterclass from those who run it as a one-off event.
