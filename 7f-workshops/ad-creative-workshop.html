The Two Creative Diagnostic Metrics

Section 02 — KPIs

Hook Rate

3-sec views ÷ Impressions
POOR
<15%
OK
15-25%
TARGET
25%+
Andromeda deprioritizes ads below 15% immediately. Fitness content benchmarks higher — always aim above 25%.

Hold Rate (30s)

30-sec views ÷ Total views
POOR
<15%
OK
15-20%
TARGET
20%+
Low hook rate = top-of-funnel problem. Low hold rate = body problem. Fix the re-hook at 10-12s and move CTA to 30-45s.

Diagnostic rule: Low hook rate = rewrite the first 3 seconds only. Low hold rate = add a re-hook at 10-12 seconds AND move the CTA earlier. They are different problems with different fixes.

Re-Hook Rule
Every script must include a mandatory re-hook at 10-12 seconds to recover nearly-scrolled viewers. This alone can double your hold rate on existing winning ads.

The 4 Hook Types — Use All Four on Every Ad

Section 02 — KPIs

Most coaches use only one hook type — the verbal hook. There are actually four distinct hook layers in every ad. Using all four is the fastest single upgrade you can make to hook rate. — Aaron Martinez

Hook Type 01 — Most Important
TEST 10 VERSIONS
Banner Text Hook
The text overlay at the top of the video. Tells viewers what they stand to gain before they've committed to watching. We have never had a video without banner text outperform one that has it. Test 10 different banner text versions per ad.
Hook Type 02
Verbal Hook
The first words out of your mouth — the opening line or sentence. What most people think of as "the hook." Non-negotiable, but only one of four layers. Your opening line must name the problem, outcome, or pattern interrupt within the first 3 seconds.
Hook Type 03
What's Happening Hook (Visual)
A physical object, action, or visual element that adds intrigue before you say a word — a document, a prop, a before/after image on screen. Adds a second layer of curiosity for viewers who are watching muted or skimming. Doesn't require expensive production.
Hook Type 04
Caption Hook
The ad copy above the video. Gives scrollers who pause on text a reason to click play. Usually pulled directly from your video script with minor edits. More people read captions than most coaches realize — especially on desktop and in the feed.

Ad Kill Rules — When to Pull the Plug

Section 02 — KPIs
New Ad Kill Threshold
2× KPI
Spend 2x your cost per KPI target before killing a new ad. Target $375/call? Spend $750 first.
Existing Winner Kill Threshold
3× KPI
Give proven winners more room. A bad week ≠ a dead ad. 3x protects winners from premature cuts.
Scenario Action Logic
Hook rate under 15% Kill or Fix Hook Only The body may be fine. Swap the first 3 seconds only. Keep the same script body.
Good hook rate, low hold rate Don't Kill — Fix Body Add re-hook at 10-12s. Move CTA to 30-45s. These are different problems, not death sentences.
New ad, 2x KPI spend, no result Kill It Don't keep burning budget hoping it turns around. Make a new entity, not a variation.
Winner struggling for 1 week Wait — 3x Threshold One bad week doesn't kill a winner. Give it room before pausing.
⚙️   Critical setting: Turn OFF Audience Network and Facebook Marketplace placements in Placement Controls. Do it once — it applies to all future ads automatically.

Winning
Variations
Most coaches make the same mistake: take a winner and change the words. Same angle. Same entity. Budget cannibalizes. Neither wins.

The Four Valid Variation Types

Section 05 — Winning Variations
Type 01 — Limited Value
Hook Swap
Change only the first 3 seconds. Same body, proof, and CTA. Same entity bucket — useful for hook rate testing only, not true entity expansion.
What changes: Hook only Entity: Same
Type 02 — High Value
Format Shift
Change the entire format — talking head to skit, B-roll to interview. Keep the core message and proof. True new entity — this is what you want for Andromeda diversity.
What changes: Format bucket Entity: New
Type 03 — High Value
Angle Shift
Change the core emotion — fear to desire, or frustration to hope. Different problem, different avatar pain point. True new entity — different emotional driver = different entity ID.
What changes: Emotion + angle Entity: New
Type 04 — High Value
Presenter Swap
Change who delivers the ad — you to your partner, or add a client testimonial. Same message structure, fresh face. True new entity — different person = different creative signal.
What changes: Presenter Entity: New

The Creative Refresh SOP: Never edit a live winning ad. Editing the ad ID resets all micro-learning. Instead: duplicate the winner, modify only what you need, launch as a new ad inside the same ad set.

The Concept → Entity Hierarchy

Section 07 — Concepts vs. Entities
CONCEPT (The Big Idea)
"Fitness coaches are trading time for money because they don't have a system to get clients consistently."
Entity A — Talking Head
Jordan, direct to camera, frustration angle
Hook: "If you're still waiting for referrals as a fitness coach, you have a system problem." CTA at 35s. Emotion: frustration.
Entity B — Skit
Jordan as the "struggling coach" character, humor angle
[Jordan in gym clothes, staring at phone] "Day 47 of posting for free... still nothing." CTA at 40s. Emotion: humor + recognition.
Entity C — B-Roll + Text
Lifestyle footage + result-lead hook, desire angle
Hook: "She went from $2K to $18K/month in 90 days." Body: system breakdown with text overlay. CTA at 30s. Emotion: desire.
Entity D — Erin Version
Same concept, empathy-forward presenter, hope angle
Hook: "I used to wake up at 5am, train 12 clients, and still not know where next month's rent was coming from." Emotion: vulnerability to hope.

The rule: One concept should birth at least 3-4 real entities across different format buckets and emotional drivers. If you can only think of one entity from a concept, you haven't mapped it far enough.
