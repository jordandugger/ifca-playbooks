Step 3 — How to Use It Every Month

The planner is only useful if you update it on a monthly cadence as your numbers change.
START OF MONTH
Set Your Monthly Budget Plan
Open the planner, update your close rate and CPL with last month's actuals, and set your new monthly budget. The 70/30 split auto-updates. Screenshot or copy the output — this is your operating budget for the month. Do not deviate from it mid-month without re-running the numbers.
MID-MONTH
Scale Readiness Check
At $X spend, is the scale readiness indicator green? If yes, you can bump budget. If red, diagnose which KPI is dragging you below the ROAS floor and fix the creative first. Never scale into a broken funnel.
⚠️
The #1 budget mistake: scaling spend before the creative is proven. Scale readiness green means you've hit minimum 3× ROAS at current spend. That's the permission slip — not your gut feeling that this is the month it'll work.
END OF MONTH
Update Actuals and Reset
Pull your actual CAC, close rate, and CPL from the month and update Panel 1 with real numbers. If your actuals are worse than your inputs, your budget plan was built on fiction. Update, recalculate, and plan next month from truth.

The 70/30 Rule — Why It Matters

This isn't a suggestion. It's the structural principle that keeps your ad account from stagnating.
AllocationPurposeWhat Breaks Without It
70% → Proven WinnersRevenue generation. Scale the entities already hitting green benchmarks.Without this, you're gambling on unproven ads. No stability.
30% → New TestsCreative pipeline. Fund the experiments that become next month's 70%.Without this, winners fatigue and there's nothing in the pipeline to replace them.
0% → "Feeling Good" AdsNone. Cut them.This is where most ad budgets die — holding underperformers on hope.
✅
The 30% is not optional. The coaches who say "I'll test new creative once the current ones start dying" are always 3 weeks behind. The creative pipeline has to be continuously fed. That's what the 30% locks in.
